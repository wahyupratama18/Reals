Lecture 1

Socially Constructing EurAsia

Dr. Evi Eliyanah
Universitas Negeri Malang
Abstract:

How European and Asian identities are socially constructed? This session offers a brief introduction to fundamental theories underlying the lecture series. Students will learn about social construction and identity/subjectivity theories. These theories are fundamental in understanding that becoming European and/or Asian is a social construction; it is fluid and intersects with other identities; it is a never-ending project. Throughout the lecture series, students will be guided to understand that becoming European may have hints of Asia or the other way around; becoming Asian/European/Eurasian may intersect with becoming middle/upper/lower class; becoming Asian/European/Eurasian may mean differently in different cultural and historical contexts; becoming Asian/European/Eurasian can offer benefits or pose challenges in particular cultural and historical setting; and more importantly that becoming Asian/European/Eurasian can shape the popular culture landscape.
